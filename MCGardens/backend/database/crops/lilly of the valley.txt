--bio
Lily of the valley (Convallaria majalis) is a delicate, sweetly scented perennial plant that produces charming, bell-shaped white flowers. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America and typically blooms in the spring. Lily of the valley is often used in bouquets and flower arrangements due to its elegant appearance and fragrance. 

--how to plant
1. Choose a shady location: Lily of the valley thrives in partial to full shade. Select a spot in your garden that receives dappled sunlight or is shaded for most of the day.
2. Prepare the soil: The plant prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Amend the soil with organic matter such as compost before planting.
3. Plant rhizomes: Lily of the valley grows from underground rhizomes. Plant the rhizomes about 4-6 inches apart and 1-2 inches deep in the soil.
4. Water thoroughly: Water the newly planted rhizomes well to help them establish in the soil.
5. Mulch the area: Apply a layer of organic mulch around the plants to help retain moisture and suppress weed growth.

--how to take care
1. Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist, but not waterlogged. Water the plants deeply when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. Avoid overhead watering to prevent fungal diseases.
2. Fertilization: Feed lily of the valley with a balanced fertilizer in the spring, following the manufacturer's instructions. Avoid over-fertilization, as this can harm the plants.
3. Mulching: Replenish the mulch around the plants each spring to conserve moisture and deter weeds.
4. Pruning: Remove spent flowers to encourage continuous blooming and prevent self-seeding. Trim back any damaged or yellowing foliage as needed.
5. Division: Every few years, divide the rhizomes in the fall after the plants have finished blooming. Replant the divided rhizomes to rejuvenate the plant and encourage healthy growth.
