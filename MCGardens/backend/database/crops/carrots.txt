--bio

Carrots are biennial plants that are grown as an annual crop for their edible orange taproot. They are a versatile vegetable used in a variety of dishes and can be enjoyed raw or cooked. Carrots are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making them a healthy addition to any diet.

--how to plant

1. Choose a sunny location with well-drained soil. Carrots prefer loose, sandy soil that is free of rocks and debris.
2. Prepare the soil by loosening it to a depth of 10-12 inches and removing any weeds or large clumps.
3. Plant carrot seeds directly into the soil, about 1/4 inch deep and 1 inch apart. You can also sow the seeds in rows, spacing them 4-6 inches apart.
4. Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged to encourage germination. Carrots need regular watering to develop properly.
5. Thin out the seedlings once they reach a height of a few inches, leaving about 2-3 inches between plants to allow room for the roots to grow.
6. Mulch around the plants to help retain moisture and suppress weeds.

--how to take care

1. Water the carrot plants consistently, providing about 1 inch of water per week. Be sure to keep the soil evenly moist, especially during hot weather.
2. Fertilize the carrots with a balanced fertilizer high in phosphorus to promote root development. Apply the fertilizer according to the manufacturer's instructions.
3. Watch out for pests such as carrot rust flies and aphids, and take appropriate measures to control them if necessary.
4. Harvest the carrots when they reach a desirable size, usually around 1/2 to 3/4 inches in diameter. Gently loosen the soil around the roots and pull them out carefully to avoid damaging the taproot.
5. Store harvested carrots in a cool, dark place with high humidity to prolong their shelf life.
