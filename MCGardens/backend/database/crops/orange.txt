--bio
Oranges are citrus fruits known for their bright color, sweet flavor, and high vitamin C content. They grow on trees and are a popular fruit in many parts of the world.

--how to plant
1. Choose a sunny location with well-draining soil for planting your orange tree.
2. Dig a hole twice as wide and deep as the root ball of the tree.
3. Place the tree in the hole, making sure the graft union is above the soil line.
4. Backfill the hole with soil, water thoroughly, and add a layer of mulch around the base of the tree.
5. Water the tree regularly, especially during the growing season.

--how to take care
1. Water the orange tree regularly, providing about 1-1.5 inches of water per week, especially during hot and dry weather.
2. Fertilize the tree with a balanced fertilizer in early spring and late summer.
3. Prune the tree to remove dead or diseased branches and to maintain its shape.
4. Protect the tree from frost by covering it with a blanket or using a frost cloth when temperatures drop below freezing.
5. Harvest the oranges when they are fully ripe, usually in the winter months, by cutting or twisting them off the tree.
