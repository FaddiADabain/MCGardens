--bio
Lettuce is a leafy green vegetable that belongs to the daisy family. It is commonly grown for its delicate, crisp leaves that are used in salads and sandwiches. Lettuce comes in various types, including romaine, iceberg, and butterhead, each with its own unique texture and flavor.

--how to plant
1. Choose a sunny location with well-drained soil for planting lettuce.
2. Sow lettuce seeds directly into the soil or start with seedlings.
3. Plant seeds or seedlings about 6 inches apart in rows that are 12-18 inches apart.
4. Cover the seeds lightly with soil and water gently to keep the soil moist.
5. Keep the soil consistently moist, but not waterlogged, throughout the growing season.

--how to take care
1. Water lettuce plants regularly, providing about 1 inch of water per week.
2. Mulch around the plants to help retain moisture and suppress weeds.
3. Fertilize lettuce plants with a balanced fertilizer about every 3-4 weeks.
4. Protect lettuce from pests like slugs and snails by using organic deterrents or physical barriers.
5. Harvest lettuce leaves by cutting them off with scissors when they reach the desired size, leaving the base of the plant intact for regrowth.
