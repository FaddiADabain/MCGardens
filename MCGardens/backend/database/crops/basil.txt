--bio
Basil is a popular herb known for its strong, pungent aroma and its use in various culinary dishes. It is easy to grow and commonly used in Mediterranean cuisine.

--how to plant
1. Choose a sunny location for planting basil as it requires at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily.
2. Plant basil in well-draining soil with a pH level between 6 and 7.
3. Sow basil seeds 1/4 inch deep in the soil, or transplant seedlings 10-12 inches apart.
4. Water the basil plant regularly to keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
5. Fertilize the basil plant every 2-3 weeks with a balanced fertilizer to promote healthy growth.

--how to take care
1. Water basil plants regularly, providing about 1 inch of water per week, either through rainfall or irrigation.
2. Mulch around the base of the plants to retain moisture and suppress weed growth.
3. Pinch off the flowers that appear on the basil plant to promote leaf growth and prevent the plant from going to seed.
4. Harvest basil leaves regularly to encourage new growth and maintain the plant's shape.
5. Protect basil plants from extreme temperatures, especially frost, as they are sensitive to cold conditions.
