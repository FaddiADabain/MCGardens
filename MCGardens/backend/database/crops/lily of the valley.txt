--bio
Lily of the valley, or Convallaria majalis, is a fragrant and delicate perennial plant known for its small white, bell-shaped flowers that hang from slender stems. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its beauty and sweet scent. Despite its charming appearance, all parts of the plant are highly toxic if ingested, so caution should be exercised when planting and caring for lily of the valley.

--how to plant
1. Choose a shady location with moist, well-draining soil for planting lily of the valley.
2. Dig a hole slightly larger than the plant's root ball and place the plant in the hole.
3. Backfill the hole with soil and water thoroughly to help establish the plant.
4. Space multiple lily of the valley plants about 6-8 inches apart to allow for spreading.

--how to take care
1. Water lily of the valley regularly, keeping the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
2. Mulch around the plants to help retain moisture and suppress weeds.
3. Fertilize lily of the valley with a balanced fertilizer once in early spring and again in late summer.
4. Remove spent flowers to encourage continuous blooming and prevent self-seeding.
5. Divide overcrowded clumps of lily of the valley every 3-4 years to maintain plant health and vigor.
